# LeonidaHQ Design Direction

## North Star

LeonidaHQ should feel like the cleanest GTA 6 intelligence desk on the internet: fast, visual, source-aware, and built for people who will check it every week for years.

## The Future Designer Council

1. Trust beats glow.
   Every flashy moment must help a visitor understand what is confirmed, what is evidence-backed, and what is theory.

2. The map is the moat.
   The site should keep pushing users toward the living Leonida map, evidence pins, and source trails. Articles support the map; the map should not feel like a side page.

3. Less cyberpunk demo, more fan intelligence system.
   Neon is the accent, not the whole meal. Avoid fake dashboards, fake AI panels, fake enterprise UI, and generic "future app" art.

4. Mobile first means article first.
   On phones, remove walls of filters, giant decorative sections, and repeated CTAs before the user reaches the content.

5. Label the claim before selling the hype.
   Use simple badges: Official, Evidence, Theory, Leak Watch, Analysis. This is how LeonidaHQ becomes trusted instead of noisy.

6. One screen, one job.
   Each section should have one clear purpose: read, open map, follow source, join list, or compare evidence.

7. Ownable beats pretty.
   The brand moat is not just pink and teal. The ownable pattern is: GTA 6 signal -> evidence status -> map location -> @viraltbf distribution.

## Design Rules

- Use black, white, hot pink, teal, and small yellow/orange warning accents.
- Keep corners at 8px unless framing a hero image.
- Use Orbitron only for labels and short headlines. Use normal body text for reading.
- Avoid emoji and broken symbols in production UI.
- Do not use fake product screenshots as article thumbnails.
- Put evidence labels near every article title.
- Push "Open Map" as the primary action on homepage and evidence content.
- Keep mobile pages short before the first useful item.
