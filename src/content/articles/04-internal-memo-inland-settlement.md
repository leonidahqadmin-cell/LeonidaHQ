Rockstar hasn't said it out loud. But we've got the proof. Leonida has **two cities**, not one.

This is the biggest under-reported feature of GTA 6, and most outlets are still treating Vice City as the only urban area. They're wrong. Here's what we've confirmed.

## What we know

**It exists.** Trailer 2 flyover footage clearly shows a metropolitan area roughly 140 miles north-northwest of Vice City. We measured it. The footprint is consistent with a city of 400,000-600,000 residents.

**It looks different from Vice City.** Where Vice City is art deco coastal tropical, this place is mid-century Southern administrative. Stone and brick. Government buildings. Less neon, more concrete.

**The two cities are connected.** A four-lane interior highway runs the corridor. We've identified at least two intermediate towns along the route.

**The economies are different.** Vice City runs on tourism and informal cash. The inland city's building stock points to government, education, agribusiness administration, and probably aerospace contracting.

## What we don't know

- **The name.** Two foreign press leaks have mentioned "Roxwood" but we can't corroborate. We won't commit until Rockstar does.
- **Whether it's fully playable** or just a backdrop. Our best guess is fully playable with lower side-activity density than Vice City.
- **The cultural relationship to Vice City.** Hostile (the historical Tallahassee-Miami pattern) or indifferent (the Orlando-Miami pattern). Could matter for storyline.

## Why this matters for you

**Don't lock into a Vice City-only operational plan.** You'll miss half the state's urban content.

**If this is the state capital — and it probably is — that's where the financial, regulatory, and law enforcement power sits.** Players who only operate in Vice City are operating in the periphery of where the actual power is.

**The transit corridor is going to be its own economy.** Interstate logistics, mobile vendors, fence pickups in the gap between law enforcement jurisdictions. Watch for it.

## The doubt

There's one possibility we have to address: Rockstar might be cutting the inland city for performance. Every disclosure so far has been incidental — flyover backgrounds, wide shots, passing mentions. No direct showcase.

Three scenarios, with our probability estimates:

- **60%:** Fully implemented, Rockstar is holding it as a launch-day reveal
- **30%:** Partially implemented, certain neighborhoods playable, others backdrop only
- **10%:** Being cut for performance, current footage doesn't reflect the distribution build

We'll revise these probabilities the moment Trailer 3 drops.

## Civilian guidance

Plan for a multi-city map. Build flexibility into your playthrough strategy from hour one. And be skeptical of every outlet that talks about "Vice City" without acknowledging there's a whole second city out there.

We're tracking every disclosure. We'll update you the moment Rockstar opens up.
