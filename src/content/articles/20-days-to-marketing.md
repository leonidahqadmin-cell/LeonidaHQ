Take-Two just removed all the mystery around timing. In the latest comments, CEO Strauss Zelnick effectively set a hard window: GTA 6 marketing begins "when summer hits" — late June or early July at the latest.

For an account that has said almost nothing for years, this is loud.

## What "Summer" Actually Means Here

Rockstar's historical pattern is one major trailer per year on the path to launch. Trailer 1 (Dec 2023), Trailer 2 (Dec 2025). The next logical drop lands squarely in that June–August band.

Zelnick's wording wasn't accidental. He didn't say "later this year." He said summer. That clusters three big moments:

1. **Trailer 3** — The one that finally shows real gameplay slices, more of Jason's voice, and confirmation (or strong visual proof) of the second city in Leonida.
2. **Pre-order opening** — The store page going live. Price reveal likely attached or immediate follow-up.
3. **The marketing machine** — Full asset drop: key art, TV spots, social push, influencer seeding.

If they hit all three in the same 4–6 week window, the hype curve will be steeper than anything we've seen since the first trailer.

## The 20-Day Implication

We're already inside the window. "At least 20 days left until the GTA 6 Launch Marketing officially kicks off" is the current read from the sharpest countdown accounts. That puts the earliest credible drop around late June.

If Rockstar follows their own playbook, we should see:

- Subtle teases first (Discord activity, small asset drops, "something is coming" energy).
- Then the big coordinated push.
- Pre-order numbers will be the first real public signal of how big this actually is.

## What Changes for You

The quiet period is ending. The next 30–45 days will be the highest-signal stretch before launch. Every Rockstar or Take-Two comment will carry weight. Leaks will spike. The second city debate will get settled one way or the other.

This is exactly why the map exists now in preview. Start learning the geography while the noise is still manageable. When the first real trailer drops, you'll already know where things are happening.

## What We Won't Get (Yet)

- Full Online reveal. Saved for post-launch or a dedicated event.
- PC date or cross-gen details.
- Exact price until the pre-order page or a separate earnings call.

Everything else is fair game.
