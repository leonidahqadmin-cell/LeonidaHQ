One of the most common GTA 6 questions has a frustrating answer: **a PC version is almost certainly coming — just not at launch.**

## What's Confirmed

Rockstar has confirmed **GTA 6 releases November 19, 2026 on PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X|S.** There is **no announced PC version** and no PC release date. That's the entire confirmed picture — anything more specific is an estimate.

## Why PC Is Almost Certain (Just Later)

Rockstar's recent pattern is consistent: ship on console first, bring the PC version months later. **GTA V** launched on console in September 2013 and didn't reach PC until April 2015 — roughly **18 months later**. **Red Dead Redemption 2** launched on console in October 2018 and arrived on PC in November 2019 — about **13 months later**.

Applying that history to GTA 6 points to a **PC release somewhere in the second half of 2027 to early 2028**, likely 6–18 months after the November 2026 console launch. The PC version also tends to be the definitive one, with higher frame rates, resolution options, and eventually mod support.

## The Honest Caveat

None of this is confirmed. Rockstar could announce PC alongside console, delay it further, or surprise everyone. But betting against a GTA 6 PC release would be betting against Rockstar's entire modern track record — and the PC market is far too large to ignore.

If you're a PC player: the game is coming. Just be ready to wait a while after your console friends.
