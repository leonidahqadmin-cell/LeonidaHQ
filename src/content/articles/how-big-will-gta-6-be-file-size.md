How much storage will you need to clear for **GTA 6**? Nothing's official yet, but the rumors — and basic logic — suggest you should start making room now.

## What the Rumors Say

Various leaks and estimates have floated eye-watering install sizes for GTA 6. None are confirmed, so treat any specific number you see today as a rumor, not a spec sheet. The direction, though, is clear: this game will be big.

## Why It Will Be Huge Regardless

Consider the context. Red Dead Redemption 2 runs around 150GB, and modern AAA games routinely cross 100GB thanks to high-resolution textures, dense worlds, and uncompressed audio. GTA 6 — a bigger, more detailed world than anything Rockstar has built — is almost certain to be one of the largest installs on console, and a day-one patch will likely add even more.

## How to Prepare

- Budget for a large install — clearing 150GB or more is a safe assumption.
- Consider a fast external or expansion SSD if your console is tight on space.
- Expect a sizable day-one update on top of the base download.

The exact figure will come from Rockstar closer to launch. Until then, the smart move is simply to make space.
