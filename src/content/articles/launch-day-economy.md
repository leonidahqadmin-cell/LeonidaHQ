Rockstar has never shipped a game this expensive before launch. That money has to come back through GTA Online 2.0.

## The Infrastructure Already on the Map

Every business, fence, and heist prep location we've plotted is a revenue node:

- Buyable franchises that generate daily cash for players who control them.
- Smuggling routes between the two cities.
- High-stakes gambling and entertainment venues that will host player events.
- Hidden locations perfect for player-run criminal empires.

This is the skeleton of a player-driven economy that can sustain itself for years without constant new map drops.

## What "Two Cities" Means for Monetization

One city was manageable. Two cities + the space between them means Rockstar can:

- Run separate seasonal events in each metro.
- Create natural rivalries between player crews based on geography.
- Gate high-value content behind travel and coordination.

The map preview is the first public look at how seriously they're taking this. The density isn't an accident.

## The Long Game

GTA V Online is still printing money 12+ years later. Leonida is built to do the same at 2-3x the scale.

The locations we add between now and launch are the ones that will define the first year of player economies.
