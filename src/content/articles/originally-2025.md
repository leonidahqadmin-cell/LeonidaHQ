It's easy to forget now, but **GTA 6 was first aimed at 2025.** The road from that early target to **November 19, 2026** is worth laying out clearly, because the shifts weren't random.

## The Timeline

When Rockstar revealed GTA 6 with its first trailer, the studio attached a broad **2025** window. Over the following months that target narrowed and then moved: from a general 2025 release, to a **fall 2025** window, to an early-2026 date, and finally to the confirmed **November 19, 2026** launch on PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X|S.

## Why It Kept Moving

None of this is unusual for a project this size. Each shift bought Rockstar more time to polish the largest open world it has ever built — and a company that ships a generation-defining game roughly once a decade has earned the patience. Big delays on flagship titles are the norm, not the exception, across the entire industry.

## Where It Stands

The date is now firm and, importantly, tied to Take-Two's financial guidance — a far stronger anchor than an early marketing window ever was. The wait got longer than fans hoped, but the destination is finally locked.
