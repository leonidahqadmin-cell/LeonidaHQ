Sony's State of Play came and went without GTA 6. That is not shocking, and it is not a real delay signal.

Rockstar has spent this cycle controlling the oxygen around the game. A surprise appearance inside someone else's showcase would have been louder, but less controlled. For GTA 6, control matters.

## What We Know

- GTA 6 did not appear at the State of Play broadcast.
- Take-Two has publicly pointed to a summer marketing window.
- Rockstar has started small infrastructure moves around GTA 6, including community-channel preparation.
- No official Trailer 3 date has been announced.

Those are the facts worth keeping. Everything else needs a confidence label.

## What We Think

The next meaningful GTA 6 move is more likely to come through Rockstar or Take-Two than through a random partner showcase.

That could mean a standalone trailer, store-page movement, new screenshots, a controlled press beat, or a short asset wave that starts quietly and builds over several days.

The important part is not "which show will they hijack?" The important part is that the parent-company language, platform preparation, and community infrastructure are all starting to point in the same direction.

## What Is Rumor

Any exact Trailer 3 date is still rumor unless Rockstar says it.

Predictions around late June or early July may be reasonable, but they are not confirmed. Treat them as signal watch, not calendar fact.

## Why It Matters

The community keeps burning energy on every Sony, Xbox, or Geoff Keighley event. That creates noise. Noise is where bad GTA 6 accounts farm panic and fake certainty.

LeonidaHQ should do the opposite: track the official window, mark theories clearly, and connect every major claim to a source, a map pin, or a visible piece of evidence.

The map does not care about showcase drama. It cares about geography, trailers, and the places Rockstar has already shown. That is where the better signal lives.
